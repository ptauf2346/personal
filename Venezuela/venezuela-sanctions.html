2 Types of Economic Warfare

  1. Capital-sponsored: Capital flight, capital strike, and normal capitalism (competition and the economic war of everyone aginst everyone)
    • Against countries that aim to undermine the 'normal' function of capitalism: anti-capitalist, socialist projects
  2. State-sponsored: State targeting of another country's economy via sanctions
    • Only particularly economically powerful states can do this (USA)
    • Targeted against countries that undermine the imperial ambitions of the powerful

Types of Sanctions Imposed on Venezuela

[A list of US Sanctions]

  • May 2006 - sanctions on arms puchases (also halting Venezuelan requests for loans from IFIs, forcing it to go to the international bond markets and to bilateral loans)

    • Guardian Article 15 May 2006

  • May 2011 - PDVSA prohibited from competing for US government procurement contracts, from securing financing from the Export-Import Bank of the United States, and from obtaining US export licenses

    • US Department of State 24 May 2011

  • March 2015 - individuals sanctions against government offficials (now 11 officials) - declaration that Venezuela represents "an unusual and extradordinary threat to national security of the United States

    • White House Executive Order 9 March 2015

  • August 2017 - Sanctions on debt transactions
  • January 2019 - Recognition of Juan Guaido as "Interim President"
    • PDVSA sanctions & take-over of Venezuelan assets in the US, including Citgo (which provided about 14 billion per year in dividends)

Effects of sanctions

  1. August 2017 on trading in Venezuela debt prevented a restructuring of its debt
  2. Decline in oil production at a three times faster rate than previously
    • 36.4% drop in oil production this year alone [07/2019], by 431,000 barrels per day, meaning another loss of $6.8 billion in 2019 (17 billion, according to F. Rodriguez)
    • Reduced exports mean reduced imports
    • Venezuela imports nearly everything that is needed for electricity generation, water treatment, and transportation
  3. Closing of Venezuelan foreign bank accounts after US gov't issued a warning in Sept 2017 to banks that they would be aiding in money laundering and corruption and would be liable
    • --> Secondary sanctions - on countries doing business with Venezuela, such as India

Economic Effects of the 2019 Sanctions

  • Cut off Venezuela from its main export market (the US) - loss over 1/3 of its exports
  • Imports of goods are projected to fall by 39.4%, from $10 billion to $6.1 billion in 2019
    • Food imports have dropped sharply along with overall imports; in 2018 they were estimated at just $2.46 billion, as compared with $11.2 billion in 2013.
  • Hyperinflation - inflation was nearing, 1,000% in 2017, pushed into hyperinflation in late 2017, after the snactions hit (over 13,000%)
  • Inability to access blocked assets:
    • $9 billion in gold reserves that cannot be accessed or sold
    • $3.4 billion in trade credits
    • $5.2 billion value of Citgo
    • $1.2 billion in gold that was held at the Bank of England
  • No access to correspondent banks used for imports of food and medicines

Effects of the Sanctions on Venezuelan Society

  • Infrastructure
    • Power and water outages
      • limited diesel fuel to power backup generators, no access to replacement parts
      • Venezuela losing a total of 6.4 percent of GDP in 2019 from the Feb national power outage and its aftermath
    • Public transportation - no access to replacement parts for imported busses or cars
  • Disease and mortality
    • Mortality increased by 31% between 2017 and 2018 - 40,000 additional deaths
    • 300,000 Venezuelans at risk because of lack of access to medicines (including 80,000 people with HIV, 4 million with diabetes, 16,000 with cancer)
    • According to the Venezuelan Medical Federation, some 22,000 doctors - about one third of the total - have left the country
  • Migration
    • UN estimates 3.5 million have left the country in the past 2-3 years
