Congressional Research Service Document overview of sanctions.

  • May 2006 - sanctions on arms puchases (also halting Venezuelan requests for loans from IFIs, forcing it to go to the international bond markets and to bilateral loans)

    • Guardian Article 15 May 2006

  • May 2011 - PDVSA prohibited from competing for US government procurement contracts, from securing financing from the Export-Import Bank of the United States, and from obtaining US export licenses

    • US Department of State 24 May 2011

  • March 2015 - individuals sanctions against government offficials (now 11 officials) - declaration that Venezuela represents "an unusual and extradordinary threat to national security of the United States

    • White House Executive Order 9 March 2015

  • August 2017 - Sanctions on debt transactions

    • NYT 25 August 2017

  • January 2019 - Recognition of Juan Guaido as "Interim President"

    • White House 23 January 2019

    • PDVSA sanctions & take-over of Venezuelan assets in the US, including Citgo (which provided about 14 billion per year in dividends)

      • US Treasury 28 January 2019

  • TradingEconomics.com This website provides historical data/forcasts on numerous different indicators. The economic and business websites/journals are typically good sources of information as the business community requires reliable data to trade and etc. Luckily they include both 'Crude Oil Production' and 'Crude Oil Rigs' as indicators that you can look at. Furthermore you can select the 'Compare' tab on the top of the graphs to pull up 2 countries at a time. Unfortunately the website prompted me to pay for an acount when I tried to look at the data myself, but at the top of the page a link indicates that this data is sourced from the US Energy Information Administration.
  • The US Energy Information Administration actually has a summary page providing crude oil production graphs for Venezuela. In fact, in Figure 1. the timing of US sanctions imposed on Venezuela are marked on the graph. However, be aware that the data in the figure seems to be an average over the year, so it is less detailed than in the video. There does appear to be some data you can download but I have yet to check it out further.
  • OPEC also has some data on Venezuela oil production in the form of yearly reports that are rather difficult to navigate through and find. I have included the link to the 2019 data but also reaffirms a note from the video that "Venezuela's oil revenues account for 99 per cent of export earnings."
